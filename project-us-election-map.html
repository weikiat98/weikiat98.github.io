Project Overview

This project visualizes 60 years of US Presidential Election history through animated choropleth maps. Using a tile grid map layout that maintains equal visual weight for each state regardless of geographic size, the animations reveal fascinating patterns in American political preferences over time.

The visualization includes three main animations: the election results map showing which party won each state, plus separate Democrat and Republican vote distribution heatmaps that illustrate the intensity of support across the nation.

Created during the election season, this project demonstrates how data visualization can make complex historical patterns immediately accessible and engaging.

Key Insights

01

California's Democratic Stronghold

California has been a historically Democratic state, with notable exceptions during the Nixon and Reagan eras. The state's political transformation from a swing state to a reliable Democratic stronghold is one of the most significant shifts in modern American politics.

02

The Nixon & Reagan Landslides

The Nixon era (1972) and Reagan era (1984) represent the only times in this dataset where Republicans nearly swept the entire country. These historic landslide victories paint the map almost entirely red, showcasing the remarkable breadth of their electoral coalitions.

Vote Distribution Heatmaps

Beyond just showing winners, these heatmaps visualize the raw vote counts for each party by state. Darker colors indicate higher vote totals, revealing population centers and strongholds for each party.
